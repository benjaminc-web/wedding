Details!

Where

The ceremony will take place at 1700 at Kolegialna 9, Płock at the registry office.

This will be followed by the reception at Różany Dworek Wedding House, Wola Łącka 10, Łącka.

Once the Polish capital , Płock, located in central Poland along the Vistula River, is a historic city known for its rich cultural heritage, medieval architecture, and vibrant atmosphere.

When

The wedding is set for Saturday, 3rd August 2024, promising a day filled with joy and celebration.

Wear

Please wear formal attire. Prepare to dazzle in your finest suits and dresses as we come together to celebrate this joyous occasion.

It's a day to dress to impress and create beautiful memories that will last a lifetime. Just a friendly reminder: we're aiming for smart shoes only, no trainers please!

Transport!

By plane

The best airports to get to Płock are either Warsaw Chopin Airport (WAW) or Warsaw Modlin (WMI). From Warsaw, you can get a direct train to Kutno and change to Płock.

For guests travelling from England here are some options on Friday and Saturday to both of Warsaw's airports.

From Warsaw Chopin (WAW), you can catch the train to Warszawa Zachodnia station and from there to Kutno, where you will change to Płock. More info here.

From Warsaw Modlin (WMI) more info here.

By car

If you prefer the flexibility of traveling by car, reaching Płock is convenient as it is well-connected by roadways.

From Warsaw to Płock it takes roughly 1.5 hours by car.

Questions?

1. What is the dress code for the wedding?

For the joyous occasion, we encourage you to embrace formal attire, allowing you to showcase your elegant style. However, above all, prioritize your comfort. Feel free to dress to impress while incorporating your unique personal touch, making the celebration even more special with your individuality and charm.

2. Are children allowed at the wedding?

Absolutely, children are more than welcome to be part of our celebration. Just to note, we kindly ask for no disruption during the ceremony.

3. What time should guests arrive for the ceremony?

Plan to arrive 20 minutes before the ceremony, which kicks off at 1700. We can't wait to share this special moment with you!

4. Can I take photos during the ceremony and reception?

We like photos, but to ensure everything runs smoothly, we kindly ask that you avoid obstructing the photographer's work and remember to keep your phones muted with no flashes on your cameras. Thank you for helping us preserve these precious memories!

5. Will there be vegetarian or special dietary options available?

Rest assured, we've got your dietary needs covered. Vegetarian and special dietary options will be available—please let us know if you have any specific requirements.

6. Is there a gift registry, and where can I find it?

In Polish culture, the tradition of presenting money in a envelope as a heartfelt gesture stands in contrast to English weddings, where the focus often revolves around the exchange of various gifts.

7. How can I RSVP to the wedding?

RSVPing is easy! Just message or WhatsApp us!

8. Is there a shuttle service provided to and from the venue?

While we don't provide a shuttle service to the ceremony, we are organising some minibuses from the ceremony to the reception and from the reception into the city center at the end of evening. More details to follow.

9. Are there any specific traditions or customs planned for the wedding?

Get ready to join in the fun! We have a few special traditions planned, and we can't wait for you to be a part of them. More details to follow!
